# Market Analysis

## 1. Positioning

ProofPitch sits between three existing categories:

- AI presentation tools.
- Sales enablement tools.
- Research and source-verification tools.

The product should not compete head-on with PowerPoint, Google Slides, Canva, Gamma, or enterprise sales enablement suites. The credible wedge is narrower: evidence-backed narrative generation for teams that need a reusable pitch pack, not a full design suite.

## 2. Market Signals

AI adoption is now broad enough that buyers understand AI-assisted content generation. McKinsey's 2025 global survey says 23% of respondents report their organizations are scaling agentic AI somewhere in the enterprise, and another 39% are experimenting with agents. This supports demand for workflow-specific AI tools, but also means generic AI claims are no longer differentiated.

Venture and sales teams both face high communication pressure. PitchBook-NVCA describes its Venture Monitor as an authoritative quarterly report on US venture activity and highlights changing market mechanics in Q1 2026. In parallel, DocSend's Startup Index shows investors often spend only a few minutes reviewing pitch decks. The practical implication: pitch materials need to communicate quickly and defensibly.

Sales collateral is also a productivity problem. Salesforce reported that sales reps spend just 28% of their time selling, implying a large amount of work is still absorbed by prep, admin, research, and content handling.

Adjacent software categories are large enough to support a focused entrant:

- Grand View Research estimates the sales enablement platform market at $5.23B in 2024, projected to $12.78B by 2030.
- Intent Market Research estimates presentation software at $9.1B in 2024, projected to $19.5B by 2030.

These are not ProofPitch's immediate market. They show adjacent budget pools. The realistic initial market is a small slice of teams that already pay for presentation, sales, or AI workflow tools.

## 3. ICP

Best first ICP:

- B2B software teams with 2 to 50 employees.
- Founder-led sales motion.
- Complex product that needs explanation.
- Existing pressure to create investor, website, sales, and launch copy.
- Willingness to pay for speed and credibility.

Strong secondary ICP:

- Agencies and consultants packaging positioning for multiple clients.
- Accelerators and venture studios that need repeatable portfolio-company materials.
- Product marketers at small and mid-market SaaS companies.

Weak initial ICP:

- Consumer creators who only need pretty slides.
- Large enterprises that require deep approval workflows before any content tool is usable.
- Students or one-off users with low willingness to pay.

## 4. Jobs To Be Done

Functional jobs:

- Turn rough notes into a coherent pitch.
- Identify unsupported claims before material is shared.
- Generate reusable copy across channels.
- Create a proof trail for internal and external trust.

Emotional jobs:

- Feel confident that the pitch is not overclaiming.
- Avoid staring at a blank deck.
- Avoid sending generic AI copy that sounds polished but shallow.

Economic jobs:

- Reduce founder and GTM prep time.
- Improve reuse of source material.
- Reduce agency or contractor spend for first-draft positioning.

## 5. Competitor Map

| Category | Examples | Strength | Gap ProofPitch Can Own |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Presentation suites | PowerPoint, Google Slides, Canva, Pitch | Familiar creation and collaboration | They do not verify product claims by default. |
| AI presentation tools | Gamma, Beautiful.ai, Tome-style tools | Fast layout and first drafts | Often optimize presentation form over claim provenance. |
| Investor document sharing | DocSend, Papermark | Analytics and distribution | They track reader behavior after content exists. |
| Sales enablement suites | Highspot, Seismic, Showpad | Enterprise content operations | Heavyweight and not founder-first. |
| Research assistants | Perplexity, Tavily-backed workflows | Fast sourcing | They do not package a complete pitch pack workflow. |
| Agencies/consultants | Positioning and deck consultants | High-touch expertise | Expensive, slower, and hard to scale across iterations. |

## 6. Differentiation

ProofPitch should differentiate on:

- Claim ledger as first-class output.
- Source-aware generation.
- One workflow from rough note to reusable pitch pack.
- Fallback-first reliability.
- Exportable artifacts rather than locked-in content.
- Clear visual distinction between supported and unsupported claims.

The strongest defensible product surface is not "AI makes decks." It is "ProofPitch prevents unverified pitch claims from becoming customer-facing material."

## 7. TAM, SAM, SOM

TAM:

- Adjacent spend includes presentation software and sales enablement software.
- These categories are multi-billion-dollar markets, but they are too broad to use as an operating plan.

SAM:

- Founder-led B2B teams, agencies, accelerators, and small GTM teams that already buy AI, presentation, research, or sales tools.
- A realistic early SAM model is 100k to 250k reachable English/French-speaking teams with annual willingness to pay between $300 and $1,000.
- That implies a practical early SAM of roughly $30M to $250M ARR, depending on channel and conversion.

SOM, first 24 months:

- Conservative: 500 paying accounts at $500 ARR = $250k ARR.
- Base: 1,500 paying accounts at $700 ARR = $1.05M ARR.
- Upside: 3,000 paying accounts at $900 ARR plus agency accounts = $3M+ ARR.

These are planning assumptions, not market facts. The first six months should test whether users pay for verification, not just generation.

## 8. Market Risks

- AI presentation tools may add source citations and claim checks.
- Users may perceive pitch generation as a one-time need.
- The product could become too horizontal if it serves founders, sales, agencies, and investors at once.
- Media generation may be visually impressive but low-retention.
- Source-backed verification can create trust risk if it overstates certainty.

## 9. Recommended Market Wedge

Start with founders and founder-led B2B teams:

- Clear pain.
- Short sales cycle.
- Strong urgency around launches, fundraising, and sales calls.
- Easy to reach through founder communities, agencies, and accelerator networks.

Then expand to agencies and accelerators because they have repeat usage across multiple companies.

Enterprise sales enablement should wait until persistence, approvals, audit logs, and security posture are mature.
